How to File GSTR-1?

🏛️ Taxation ⚡ Intermediate ⏱ 15 min read ✓ Updated Apr 2026

GSTR-1 is the monthly or quarterly statement of outward supplies every registered GST taxpayer must file. This guide walks you through the complete process — table by table — with no jargon and no confusion.

Prerequisites — Before You Begin

  • Active GSTIN (GST Identification Number) for your business
  • Sales invoices for the filing period (month or quarter)
  • Customer GSTINs for all B2B transactions
  • Login credentials for the GST Portal (gst.gov.in)
  • EVC (Electronic Verification Code) or DSC (Digital Signature Certificate) for filing
  • Knowledge of your filing frequency — Monthly or Quarterly (QRMP Scheme)
Exhibit A · Overview

What is GSTR-1?

GSTR-1 is a statement of outward supplies — a return filed by every registered GST taxpayer detailing all the sales and supplies made during a given period. It is not a tax payment return; it simply reports what you sold, to whom, and at what tax rate.

Information filed in your GSTR-1 directly flows into the GSTR-2B of your buyers, which they use to claim Input Tax Credit (ITC). This is why filing GSTR-1 accurately and on time is critical — not just for your compliance, but for your customer's ITC eligibility.

📋 Who Must File GSTR-1?

Every GST-registered business with regular taxpayer status must file GSTR-1. Composition taxpayers, Input Service Distributors (ISD), and certain non-resident taxpayers file different returns and are not required to file GSTR-1.

Exhibit B · Frequency & Deadlines

Filing Frequency & Due Dates

Your GSTR-1 filing frequency depends on your annual aggregate turnover in the previous financial year.

Annual Turnover Frequency Due Date Scheme
Up to ₹5 Crore Quarterly 13th of the month following the quarter QRMP Scheme (Optional)
Above ₹5 Crore Monthly 11th of the following month Regular
⚠️ Late Filing Penalty

Late filing attracts a penalty of ₹50 per day (₹25 CGST + ₹25 SGST) up to ₹10,000. For nil returns (no supplies), the late fee is ₹20 per day (₹10 CGST + ₹10 SGST). Interest on late tax payment is charged at 18% per annum — but GSTR-1 itself has no tax payment, so interest does not apply directly to this return.

Exhibit C · Field Procedure

Step-by-Step: Filing GSTR-1

1
Step One
Log in to the GST Portal

Go to gst.gov.in. Click Login at the top right. Enter your Username and Password. Complete the CAPTCHA and sign in.

Once logged in, you will land on your taxpayer dashboard. Confirm the GSTIN shown matches the entity you are filing for.

2
Step Two
Navigate to the Returns Dashboard

From the top navigation, go to: Services → Returns → Returns Dashboard

On the Returns Dashboard, select the Financial Year and the Return Filing Period (the month or quarter you are filing for). Click Search.

You will see a set of return tiles. Click Prepare Online under the GSTR-1 tile to begin filling your return.

3
Step Three
Fill Table 4 — B2B Invoices (Taxable Outward Supplies to Registered Persons)

This is the most important table. Report every invoice raised to a GST-registered buyer (another business with a GSTIN).

  • Click B2B Invoices on the GSTR-1 dashboard.
  • Add the Receiver's GSTIN, Invoice Number, Invoice Date, Invoice Value, Place of Supply, Reverse Charge (Yes/No), and Taxable Value.
  • Select the applicable tax rate (IGST / CGST+SGST). The portal auto-calculates the tax amount.
  • Save each invoice. You can upload invoices in bulk using the Download Template → fill → Upload JSON method.
💡 GSTIN Validation

The portal validates the buyer's GSTIN in real-time. If the GSTIN is invalid, the invoice will not save. Always verify GSTINs at Search Taxpayer before raising invoices.

4
Step Four
Fill Table 5 — B2C (Large) Invoices

B2C Large means: supplies made to unregistered persons (consumers/individuals) where the invoice value exceeds ₹2.5 lakh in inter-state supplies.

  • Click B2CL (Large) Invoices.
  • Enter Invoice Number, Date, Value, Place of Supply, and Tax details.
  • Only inter-state supplies above ₹2.5 lakh come here. Intra-state B2C supplies go to Table 7 regardless of invoice value.
5
Step Five
Fill Table 7 — B2C (Small) Summary

All remaining B2C supplies — intra-state B2C of any value, and inter-state B2C invoices below ₹2.5 lakh — are reported here as a state-wise aggregate. You do not need invoice-level details for this table.

  • Select the Place of Supply (state/UT code).
  • Enter the total taxable value and applicable tax rate.
  • Repeat for each state where you made B2C supplies.
6
Step Six
Fill Other Applicable Tables

Depending on your business, you may also need to fill:

TablePurpose
Table 6AExports (with or without payment of tax)
Table 6BSupplies to SEZ units / developers
Table 6CDeemed exports
Table 8Nil rated, exempted, and non-GST outward supplies
Table 9Amendments to B2B invoices of previous tax periods
Table 10Amendments to B2CL invoices of previous tax periods
Table 11Advances received / adjusted (applicable for certain taxpayers)
Table 12HSN-wise summary of outward supplies
Table 13Documents issued (invoices, credit notes, debit notes, etc.)
⚠️ HSN Summary (Table 12) — Mandatory

From April 2021, HSN-wise summary is mandatory. Taxpayers with turnover up to ₹5 Cr must report 4-digit HSN; above ₹5 Cr must report 6-digit HSN. Aggregate for each HSN code — do not list individual invoices.

7
Step Seven
Preview the Draft GSTR-1

Before submitting, always preview your return. Click Preview Draft GSTR-1 from the GSTR-1 dashboard. Download the PDF summary and verify:

  • Total outward taxable value matches your books
  • CGST + SGST vs IGST split is correct (intra vs inter-state)
  • All B2B GSTINs are correctly entered
  • HSN summary totals tally with invoice-level data
8
Step Eight
File GSTR-1

Once satisfied with the preview, click File GSTR-1. A confirmation popup will appear. Select your authorised signatory from the dropdown.

Choose your verification method:

  • EVC (Electronic Verification Code) — OTP sent to the registered mobile number / email. Suitable for proprietorships and individuals.
  • DSC (Digital Signature Certificate) — Required for Companies and LLPs mandatorily; optional for others.

After successful verification, an ARN (Acknowledgement Reference Number) is generated. Download and save this ARN — it is your proof of filing.

✅ Filing Confirmed

Once filed, GSTR-1 cannot be revised for that period. Corrections must be made via amendments in the subsequent period's GSTR-1 using Tables 9 and 10. Plan and verify carefully before filing.

Exhibit D · Special Cases

Filing a Nil GSTR-1

If you have no outward supplies in a given period — no sales, no exports, nothing — you still must file a Nil GSTR-1. It is mandatory regardless of business activity.

The process is identical, but faster. Log in → Returns Dashboard → GSTR-1 → Prepare Online. Since there is nothing to fill, simply click File GSTR-1 directly. Select your signatory, verify via EVC or DSC, and submit. The portal will automatically record it as a nil return.

🚫 Do Not Skip Nil Filing

Even months with zero sales require filing. Non-filing blocks your buyers from claiming ITC from you in future periods and attracts late fees. There is no exemption for nil activity.

Exhibit E · Known Errors

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Error · 01
Wrong Place of Supply
Always check whether the supply is intra-state or inter-state. Wrong POS = wrong tax (CGST/SGST vs IGST), which creates mismatches in the buyer's 2B.
Error · 02
Invalid / Mistyped GSTIN
A single digit error in the buyer's GSTIN means their ITC is blocked. Always verify GSTINs before adding them. The portal does check, but do not rely solely on it.
Error · 03
Missing HSN Summary
Table 12 is mandatory. Forgetting it causes errors on submission. Fill the HSN-wise summary even if the data seems redundant — it is a regulatory requirement.
Error · 04
Filing After Due Date
Late fees accumulate per day. If you miss the 11th or 13th deadline, file as soon as possible and pay the late fee before the system blocks future filings.
Error · 05
Not Reconciling with Books
Always reconcile GSTR-1 totals with your sales register before filing. Mismatch between GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B is a common GST scrutiny trigger.
Error · 06
Forgetting Amendments
If you filed wrong data in a previous period, use Tables 9/10/11 in the current GSTR-1 to amend. Do not re-enter the original wrong invoice — amend it properly.
Exhibit F · Summary

Key Takeaways

  • 01📋GSTR-1 is a statement of outward supplies — not a tax payment. Tax is paid via GSTR-3B.
  • 02📅Monthly filers (turnover > ₹5 Cr): file by 11th. Quarterly QRMP filers: file by 13th of the month after the quarter.
  • 03📊B2B invoices go to Table 4. Inter-state B2C > ₹2.5L go to Table 5. All other B2C go to Table 7 as aggregates.
  • 04🔢HSN-wise summary (Table 12) is mandatory for all taxpayers — 4-digit for ≤₹5 Cr, 6-digit for >₹5 Cr turnover.
  • 05✅Always preview before filing. GSTR-1 cannot be revised — only amended in a future period's return.
  • 06🔄Your GSTR-1 data flows directly to your buyer's GSTR-2B. File accurately — wrong data blocks their ITC claims.
  • 07🚫File nil GSTR-1 even if you have zero outward supplies in a period. Non-filing attracts late fees.
  • 08💾Save your ARN after every filing. It is your proof of submission and required for reconciliation and audits.
